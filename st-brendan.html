  <h2>History</h2>

  <p>Caldwell Atoll was first recorded by European explorers in the 18th century, though it was likely visited by Polynesian navigators long before. The atoll became known for its isolation and strategic location during early trans-Pacific voyages.</p>


  <h2>Geography</h2>

  <p>The atoll comprises a group of small islets surrounding a shallow central lagoon. The terrain is mostly low-lying and rocky, with limited fresh water sources.</p>


  <h2>Government</h2>

  <p>Caldwell Atoll is administered as a special territory under a larger regional governance framework. It has a local council which oversees daily matters.</p>


  <h2>Culture</h2>

  <p>Residents of the atoll maintain a mix of traditional seafaring customs and modern island life. The culture has been influenced by a blend of indigenous traditions and settler heritage.</p>


  <h2>Transportation</h2>

  <p>There is no airport on the atoll. Supply ships arrive a few times a year, and most transportation is by boat. Communications are limited but improving.</p>
